** DeepFilterNet Parameter Tuning Guide
*** --min-db-thresh (Minimum DB Threshold)

The threshold below which the filter treats audio as complete noise/silence.

- *The Danger*: If set too high (e.g., -10 dB), it will aggressively mute/slice your actual speech (especially the quiet, trailing ends of words like "s" or "t").

- *How to set*:
  1. Open the audio in Sonic Visualiser.
  2. Set the vertical Scale to *dB*.
  3. Find the peak decibel level of the speech (e.g., if peaks are around -15 dB).
  4. Set =--min-db-thresh= at least *15 to 20 dB lower* than the speech peaks (e.g., -30 dB or -35 dB) to ensure the tails of words are preserved.

*** --atten-lim-db (Attenuation Limit in dB)

Controls how aggressively background noise is reduced.

- *The Danger*: Setting this too high (e.g., 20 dB or more) can make the remaining voice sound artificial, metallic, or heavily "gated" if the background noise changes abruptly.

- *How to set*:
  - *12 dB to 15 dB (Recommended)*: Provides a very natural-sounding reduction. The background noise is pushed down cleanly without turning the background into "dead/vacuum silence".

  - *18 dB to 20 dB*: Use only for extremely noisy files where complete noise removal is prioritized over vocal naturalness.

** deepfilter-net
*** default

#+begin_src sh
deep-filter \
--model ~/.local/share/deepfilternet/DeepFilterNet3_onnx.tar.gz \
--atten-lim-db 70 \
--pf \
--pf-beta 0.05 \
-o enhanced_audio \
input.wav
#+end_src

*** atten-lim-db 35

#+begin_src sh
deep-filter \
--model ~/.local/share/deepfilternet/DeepFilterNet3_onnx.tar.gz \
--atten-lim-db 35 \
--min-db-thresh=-5 \
-D \
--pf \
--pf-beta 0.02 \
-o enhanced_audio_v2 \
input.wav
#+end_src
